If you don't see the animation, you need to download the Flash plug-in.

The demonstration clearly shows that the angle ø is identical in both triangles.

Using the sine function:

sine ø = htable ÷ ltable

for the large triangle, and

sine ø = hpuck ÷ lpuck

for the small triangle and that the angle ø is the same for both triangles we can state that:

htable ÷ ltable = hpuck ÷ lpuck

and use this relationship to determine the height of the puck above its lowest point and consequently the gravitational potential energy of the puck at its maximum height.
