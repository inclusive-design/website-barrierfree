TILE POTLUCK

June 19, 2003

Group Activity – Customization of a learning activity, through adaptation of content, or pedagogical framework.

The purpose of this activity is to explore:

  • customization of curriculum content to address a specific learning outcome, grade level or geographic location,
  • use and reuse of learning activity templates with a variety of curriculum content,
  • adaptation of learning activities for either individual learning, or large class contexts.

The group activity will be act as a catalyst for plenary discussion of what repository service functions or tools we need to support these transformations of content.

Your group is asked to develop learning activities for a specific grade or post-secondary level, and geographic location of your choosing. The following resources are provided:

  • Three learning activity templates
  • Several curriculum content samples
  • Powerpoint template for group presentation

Guidelines:

  1. Decide upon on the grade level or post-secondary level and geographic location of your learners.
    • Design three different learning activities by combining one of the content samples with each of the three activity templates.
    • Modify the PowerPoint presentation provided to create a mock up or description of each activity, to be presented to the larger group.

  2. After you have prepared three learning activities for a content area, transform each of the activities again for a second content area. Add these three additional activities to your Powerpoint presentation.

  3. Transform the learning activities once again, adapting for either individual learning (K-12) or for large class teaching (post-secondary). Add these final three additional activities to your Powerpoint presentation.
