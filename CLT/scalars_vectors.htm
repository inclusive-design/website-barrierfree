Scalars and Vectors

 

Scalar: Any quantity that can be completely described by its size, magnitude, and units is a scalar quantity; e.g., mass, energy, speed, and distance.

Vector: Any quantity that must be described by its size and a direction as well as units is a vector quantity; e.g., weight, force, velocity, displacement.

So, speed is a scalar. But, speed with direction (velocity) is a vector. Distance is a scalar, but distance with direction (displacement) is a vector. In this example the bag moves along the horizontal, its displacement is its change in position in the horizontal plane.
