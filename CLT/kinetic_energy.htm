Kinetic Energy

 

To arrive at a quantitative definition of kinetic energy, consider an object with a mass m moving with an initial speed vi. To accelerate the object uniformly to a speed vf, a constant net force F must act in the same direction as the motion over a distance d. Work is done on the object to change its speed.

1. The work done is quantified using:

W = Fd

2. Using Newton's second law F = ma substituting for F leaves:

W = mad

3. Using the kinematics equation vf2 = vi2 + 2ad and solving for ad:

2ad = vf2 - vi2
ad = (vf2 - vi2) ÷ 2

4. This can then be substituted in to get the work done to accelerate the object to speed vf:

W = m * (vf2 - vi2) ÷ 2

5. The term 1/2 (mv2) defines the kinetic energy of an object with mass m moving with speed v:

Ek = mv2
