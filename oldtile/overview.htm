Introduction

Built upon the work of the Barrierfree project, The Inclusive Learning Exchange (TILE) will establish a Learning Object Repository (LOR) service that responds to the individual needs of the learner.This project will act as an exemplar of inclusive e-learning and form the basis of extensions to international e-learning specifications. By making broadband e-learning inclusive of people with disabilities TILE will advance e-learning functionality for all learners.

The Inclusive Learning Exchange will advance LOR technology to the next level by developing the authoring tools, repository architecture, and preference schema needed to support this learner-centric transformation. The service will be implemented across Canada by a network of learning communities from many sectors. TILE will respond to individual needs of the learner, with functionality extended to other media types used in e-learning (hypertext, various xml formats, animation, simulations, Java and others) and to additional learner-centric transformations and adaptations.

The overall project goal is to serve learners in Canada who require a learner-centric approach to education using the opportunities afforded by broadband networked learning and thereby influence technical developers and international interoperability specification bodies to adopt more inclusive approaches to e-learning.

Target Audience

The developments in this project are critical to any learner or learning context that does not fit the model of the typical student and the typical learning environment. This includes learners with disabilities, with unique learning styles, using atypical client devices, learning in unusual environments and learners approaching the topic from different perspectives or knowledge backgrounds.

The Inclusive Learning Exchange includes communities of learners who have been marginalized by conventional education systems in Canada. Among these communities are learners with disabilities, learners enrolled in adult literacy programmes, transitional programmes and academic upgrading programmes . TILE will provide an opportunity to pool, share, re-purpose and collaboratively or cumulatively author accessible learning content. Through its many partners, the project will addresses learners in K to 12, in post-secondary institutions, in the workplace and adult learners seeking academic upgrading.

Project Team

A number of learning communities will participate in designing tools, developing content, implementing the service within their community and evaluating learning outcomes. These include: Frontier College, the National Adult Literacy Database, Special Needs Opportunity Windows and its network of over 20 organizations concerned with educating learners with disabilities, the Transitional Year Programme at the University of Toronto, the Independent Learning Centre, the Canadian Deafness and Training Institute, the Centre of Excellence for Research in Immigration and Settlement and Canadian Learning Television.

Infrastructure Support will be provided by the Community Access Programme and its associated Web4All programme, by providing broadband internet access points to under-serviced communities across Canada.

Formative and summative usability evaluations and learning outcome evaluations will be coordinated by Ryerson University and the Ontario Institute for Studies in Education at the University of Toronto. Accessibility evaluations will be performed by ATRC’s WebSavvy service.

WebCT will integrate the technologies developed by TILE into their software and services, Adaptive Tutoring will integrate the accessibility components developed into their product line (see descriptions above).

Summary

One of the opportunities afforded by broadband networks is a highly responsive dynamic web interface. Broadband networks open the communication channel in both directions, enabling educational servers that can respond to each individual learner and learning context in a timely manner. With the right tools this provides the opportunity to introduce a new level of learner-centric education that is inclusive of all learners.
The project will demonstrate the potential benefits of personalized courseware for the learner and the impact this would have on the educator or content producer. Through this project we hope to demonstrate the practical boundaries of transformable content and the tools needed to support the mastery of learner customizable teaching.
