The Inclusive Learning Exchange (TILE)

PROJECT PLAN

Implementation

The repository and tools will be developed by the ATRC in collaboration with Adaptive Tutoring, SUN Microsystems, and WebCT Canada. ATRC will integrate a number of learning metric approaches developed by Adaptive Tutoring into the base authoring tools and Adaptive Tutoring will make their tools accessible. Both ATRC and Adaptive Tutoring will develop their LOR services and create the necessary technical layer to allow communication between the repositories. This same common query protocol will be developed to communicate with other LOR systems in Canada and internationally. SUN Microsystems will assist in hardware integration and Java development. WebCT will integrate the technologies developed by TILE into their WebCT Web service.

Community Building and “the Community Kitchen”

Throughout the duration of the project TILE will host regular “Learning object potlucks and community kitchens” in different locations across Canada. Participants will be invited from the various learning communities. These events will introduce author/educators to the LOR service, create a community of collaborators, train participants in the use of the authoring tools and provide the opportunity to collect valuable user feedback regarding the design of the tools.

Associated with these events will be several synchronous and asynchronous forums to discuss teaching and learning approaches and best practices. TILE will employ such tools as a learning content classified to solicit available learning objects. The project site will feature different authors and learning objects on a rotating basis. There will also be reciprocal links to all of the partner maintained websites and feature articles in the periodicals and newsletters published by the partners.

Evaluation

Formative evaluations will be conducted during the design and development phase of the project. Tool prototypes will be iteratively tested by small user groups. The development cycle will be completed by beta tests involving developers, author/educators and learners. Following implementation of the service, learning outcome evaluations will be coordinated by researchers from OISE and Ryerson University.

Commercialization and Disseminatin

With a significant market developed for accessible e-learning tools and content, both WebCT and Adaptive Tutoring will address this market by integrating tools developed through TILE into their product line.

Information about the project will be disseminated through existing e-learning networks such as CREN, the NODE and international e-learning forums. The project partners will also prepare a position paper for the 2003 CMEC conference. The partners will utilize the considerable formal and informal networks available to them to disseminate information about the projects.
